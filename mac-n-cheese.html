Making Mac: A Guide

2/1/2020 | by Grant Windsor

"Macaroni cheese bake - livetocook.com" by Rob Galloway is licensed under CC BY 2.0

Some say making macaroni and cheese is not "real cooking". Whatever that means. To them I say: Codswalop. Take it from me, anyone who badmouths mac and cheese is an uncultured buffoon. I'm going to tell you why I like macaroni and cheese, and then I'm going to teach you to make it, and you better make it too.

Why is it that I love mac and cheese? Well one day when I was very young, I learned how to cook my first meal. Guess what is was? You already know. What if I had never made that first batch of Mac? Would I even be a cook today?? Doubtful. Mac 'n' Cheese is first dish of all great cooks.

You will need:

  • A Box of Macaroni and Cheese.
  • Butter
  • Milk
  • Some hotdogs

Instructions

  1. Boil a pot of water. Pour the macaroni noodles in. Don't pour the cheese bag in.

  2. When the noodles become soft pour them into a sieve. Pour them back into the pot.

  3. Put some hotdogs in the microwave. Cook them while you prepare the rest of the mac.

  4. Stir in a tablespoon of butter, 1/4 a cup of milk, and the cheese from the cheese bag over low heat. Mix it well.

  5. Cut up those hotdogs. Plunk 'em in. If you're feeling brave you can add eggs, ketchup, spinach, and beans, or eat your mac on slices of bread.
