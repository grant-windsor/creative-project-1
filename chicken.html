Chicken Breasts To Save Your Life

2/1/2020 | by Grant Windsor

"Sous Vide Chicken Breast" by sousvideguy is licensed under CC BY 2.0

Listen my friends, and let me tell you of the time I was lost in the woods with only some chicken breasts, some oil and spices, and portable stove. I was famished and it was getting dark. I decided to camp for the night. I cooked my chicken using a recipe my mom had taught me in happier times.

As I cooked, I became aware of a mysterious presence behind me. I turned and found myself face to face with the lonely man of winter. He was dressed in ragged clothes and had a long white beard. He just stood there in the snow, staring at me with eyes like deep pools of molasses.

Finally I found my voice and told him I was lost. He stood there, looking at me for a long time. Finally he pointed at the chicken I was cooking. Did he want some? I offered a piece to him, which he immediately took and ate. I gave him some more and soon my chicken was all gone. After he had eaten, he wiped his mouth on his sleeve and pointed into the sky. It took me a while to see what he was pointing at, but I eventually saw that he was pointing at a bright star, which had appeared in the dimming light. I asked him what he meant. In a strange rough voice he told me "You go, follow the star". Then he just left, disapearing into the night like a ghost. I stood awestruck for a moment, and then decided to take he old man's advice. I followed the star and soon saw the lights of a highway. I was saved.

This is the chicken I cooked for the old man:

You will need:

  • Chicken Breasts
  • Olive Oil
  • Spices (pretty much any kind as far as I can tell)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350

  2. Defrost chicken in the microwave for a couple minutes.

  3. Rub olive oil and spices onto the chicken breasts.

  4. Place the chicken breasts on a pan and cook them for about 20 minutes.

  5. When the inside is no longer pink and has reached an internal temperature of 165 degrees, they are done. Let them cool for 10 minutes before eating.
